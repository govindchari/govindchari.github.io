I did my PhD with [Dr. Behçet Açıkmeşe](https://www.aa.washington.edu/facultyfinder/behcet-acikmese) at the University of Washington, where my research focused on optimization theory and high-performance implementations of optimization algorithms. Before that, I graduated _summa cum laude_ from Cornell University in three years with a BS in Mechanical Engineering and received the [Sibley Prize](https://www.mae.cornell.edu/mae/programs/undergraduate-programs/graduation-ceremony/awards), awarded to the mechanical engineering graduate with the highest cumulative GPA.

I interned at SpaceX three times, where I worked on [Raptor](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k0t6_l3x-f8) propulsion analysis, [Dragon](https://www.spacex.com/vehicles/dragon/) guidance, navigation, and control, and [Starlink](https://www.starlink.com/) network topology. After that, I interned at [Anduril](https://www.anduril.com/) on the guidance, navigation, and control team, focusing on trajectory optimization, then at [Mitsubishi Electric Research Labs](https://www.merl.com/), where I worked with [Dr. Arvind Raghunathan](https://www.merl.com/people/raghunathan) on optimization algorithms, and finally at Amazon as an Applied Scientist intern on the [Supply Chain Optimization Technologies](https://www.amazon.science/tag/supply-chain-optimization-technologies) team.
